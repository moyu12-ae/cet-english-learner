Musician and composer Yuji Ohno's official website announced on Wednesday that Ohno died of natural causes on May 4. He was 84. Ohno's production company Office Augusta noted that "he died after going to sleep, without any change in his prior condition before going to sleep."

Ohno is best known for his work as a jazz pianist and composer. His soundtrack for the Lupin III anime series includes the iconic opening theme, which has persisted in various arrangements by Ohno throughout the franchise's many television series, specials, and films. His first involvement with the franchise was in Lupin III: Part II, the second television anime, in 1977. He performed music from the franchise in concerts over the decades as part of the jazz act Yuji Ohno & Lupintic Five, later renamed to Yuji Ohno & Lupintic Six.

Ohno began practicing piano in early childhood and taught himself jazz in high school. He was part of Keio University's Light Music Society band and played in clarinetist Koji Fujika's jazz quintet during his college years. He later formed a jazz trio with drummer Hideo Shiraki and singer Yuzo Kayama. After the trio dissolved, he focused on composing, and later scored Kon Ichikawa's mystery film The Inugami Family and Junya Sato's Proof of the Man film, both released in 1977. He also composed music for the 1978 film Yasei no Shomei, whose theme song "Senshi no Kyusoku" was covered by Soichiro Hoshi as one of the ending songs for the Heaven's Lost Property anime.

Beyond the Lupin III franchise, Ohno also composed music for Captain Future — including the insert song "Oira wa Sabishii Spaceman," later covered by Minori Chihara for the Cat Planet Cuties anime — as well as Andromeda Stories, Undersea Super Train: Marine Express, A Time Slip of 10000 Years: Prime Rose, a piece for the Daicon films from the staff that would later form studio Gainax, and the theme for the Space Adventure Cobra anime series.
